# Core Gameplay Loop

The core gameplay of Ashes of Velsingrad is built around a cycle of exploration, tactical combat, and meaningful choice-making that shapes both Kaelen's character and the world around him.

## 🔄 The Main Loop

### 1. 🌲 Exploration & Survival

Players traverse a war-torn world filled with dangers and opportunities:

**World Navigation:**
- **Overworld Map**: Navigate between regions, each with unique challenges and stories
- **Local Exploration**: Detailed exploration of villages, ruins, and wilderness areas
- **Environmental Storytelling**: Discover the world's history through visual cues and hidden details

**Resource Management:**
- **Food & Supplies**: Keep your company fed and equipped
- **Medicine**: Treat injuries and illnesses that can affect combat performance
- **Equipment**: Maintain and upgrade weapons, armor, and magical items
- **Morale**: Manage the psychological well-being of your companions

**Discovery Elements:**
- **Hidden Locations**: Secret areas with valuable loot or important story elements
- **Ancient Ruins**: Explore pre-Fall civilizations and uncover lost knowledge
- **Survivor Encounters**: Meet NPCs with their own stories, needs, and potential as allies

### 2. 📜 The Challenge Board System

Central to the game's structure is the Challenge Board, found in taverns, refugee camps, and safe zones:

**Contract Types:**

| Type | Description | Rewards | Risks |
|------|-------------|---------|-------|
| **Assassination** | Eliminate specific targets | High coin, reputation with some factions | Moral corruption, enemies |
| **Rescue** | Save captured or trapped individuals | Reputation, potential allies | Time pressure, dangerous locations |
| **Sabotage** | Disrupt enemy operations | Strategic advantages, resources | Stealth challenges, faction hostility |
| **Investigation** | Uncover secrets and solve mysteries | Information, unique items | Complex puzzles, moral dilemmas |
| **Escort** | Protect individuals during travel | Steady income, connections | Combat encounters, responsibility |
| **Bounty Hunting** | Track down criminals and fugitives | Variable rewards based on difficulty | Dangerous opponents, moral ambiguity |

**Dynamic Consequences:**
- Completed contracts affect faction standings
- Failed missions can have lasting story implications
- Some contracts become unavailable based on your moral alignment
- Success builds reputation, opening new opportunities

### 3. ⚔️ Tactical Combat

Combat in Ashes of Velsingrad emphasizes strategy over reflexes:

**Turn-Based Strategy:**
- **Initiative System**: Speed and circumstances determine turn order
- **Action Points**: Each character has limited actions per turn
- **Positioning**: Terrain, elevation, and formation are crucial
- **Environmental Hazards**: Use the battlefield to your advantage

**Combat Depth:**
- **Combo Attacks**: Chain abilities between party members
- **Elemental Interactions**: Fire spreads, water conducts electricity, earth blocks paths
- **Status Effects**: Poison, burning, fear, and corruption affect long-term strategy
- **Equipment Impact**: Different weapons and armor significantly change tactics

**Stakes and Consequences:**
- **Permanent Injury**: Characters can suffer lasting wounds
- **Death and Resurrection**: Fallen allies can be brought back through dark magic—but at what cost?
- **Reputation Impact**: How you fight affects how the world sees you

### 4. 🏕️ Company Management

Your band of survivors is more than just a combat party:

**Companion Dynamics:**
- **Individual Personalities**: Each character has unique traits, fears, and motivations
- **Loyalty System**: Actions and decisions affect how companions view Kaelen
- **Personal Quests**: Deep storylines for each major companion
- **Conflict Resolution**: Mediate disputes and manage group dynamics

**Camp Management:**
- **Base Building**: Improve your mobile camp with better equipment and facilities
- **Crafting System**: Create and upgrade equipment using gathered resources
- **Training**: Develop character abilities and learn new skills
- **Rest and Recovery**: Heal wounds, restore magic, and boost morale

**Recruitment:**
- **Iron Crows Reunion**: Search for scattered members of your former company
- **New Allies**: Recruit survivors, deserters, and others seeking purpose
- **Loyalty Tests**: Prove worthy of their trust through actions, not words

### 5. 🔥 Power Progression & Moral Alignment

Character development in Ashes of Velsingrad is tied directly to moral choices:

**Alignment Paths:**

**⚔️ Vengeance Path:**
- **Abilities**: Enhanced damage, intimidation, fear-based magic
- **Playstyle**: Aggressive tactics, ruthless efficiency
- **World Impact**: NPCs fear but respect your power
- **Corruption Risk**: High - anger feeds the curse within

**🛡️ Redemption Path:**
- **Abilities**: Healing magic, protective spells, inspiration
- **Playstyle**: Defensive strategies, minimal casualties
- **World Impact**: NPCs trust and seek your protection
- **Corruption Risk**: Low - but harder to gain raw power

**👑 Domination Path:**
- **Abilities**: Dark magic, mind control, necromancy
- **Playstyle**: Overwhelming force, fear tactics
- **World Impact**: NPCs submit but plot against you
- **Corruption Risk**: Extreme - power comes at great cost

**The Curse System:**
- **Power Level**: Stronger abilities unlock as corruption increases
- **Side Effects**: Physical and mental changes affect interactions
- **Point of No Return**: High corruption can lock you into the Domination path
- **Redemption Mechanics**: Difficult but possible to reverse corruption through sacrifice

### 6. 🏚️ Consequences & Rest Cycles

The world reacts dynamically to your choices:

**World State Changes:**
- **Faction Power Shifts**: Your actions strengthen or weaken various groups
- **Regional Stability**: Areas can become safer or more dangerous based on your involvement
- **NPC Fates**: Characters you've met continue their own stories, affected by your choices

**Rest and Reflection:**
- **Camp Conversations**: Deep discussions with companions about recent events
- **Moral Reflection**: Kaelen contemplates his actions and their implications
- **Planning Phase**: Choose which contracts to pursue and which regions to explore next

**Long-term Consequences:**
- **Ending Variations**: Multiple endings based on accumulated choices and final alignment
- **Companion Fates**: Each ally's ultimate destiny depends on your relationship and shared experiences
- **World Legacy**: The state of Velsingrad after your journey reflects your moral choices

## 🎯 Design Philosophy

### Player Agency
Every choice matters, from major story decisions to small interactions with NPCs. The game respects player decisions and builds consequences organically.

### Meaningful Difficulty
Challenges come not just from combat difficulty, but from moral complexity. The "right" choice isn't always clear, and powerful options come with significant costs.

### Emergent Storytelling
While there's a central narrative, much of the story emerges from the intersection of player choices, companion reactions, and world state changes.

### Tactical Depth
Combat should feel like solving puzzles rather than button mashing. Every encounter should offer multiple viable approaches.

## 🔄 Example Gameplay Session

Here's how a typical gameplay session might unfold:

1. **Morning in Camp**: Wake up to find a companion struggling with nightmares—do you comfort them or focus on practical concerns?

2. **Travel to Town**: En route, encounter refugees fleeing bandits—help them, ignore them, or demand payment for protection?

3. **Challenge Board**: Arrive at town to find three contracts available:
   - Hunt down a supposed witch (high pay, moral uncertainty)
   - Rescue miners trapped by a cave-in (time pressure, moderate reward)
   - Investigate strange lights in the forest (mystery, unknown rewards)

4. **Preparation**: Purchase supplies, upgrade equipment, have conversations with townspeople that reveal local politics and concerns.

5. **Mission Execution**: Complete your chosen contract, making tactical and moral decisions that affect the outcome.

6. **Consequences**: Return to find the town changed by your actions—perhaps the witch you killed was actually healing plague victims, or the miners you saved provide valuable intelligence.

7. **Evening Reflection**: Back at camp, companions react to your choices, loyalty shifts, and new conversation options open up.

8. **Planning**: Based on new information and changing circumstances, decide where to go next and what kind of leader Kaelen is becoming.

This cycle creates a rich, interconnected experience where every choice ripples outward, creating a unique story for each player while maintaining the core themes of power, responsibility, and redemption.
